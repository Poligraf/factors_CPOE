Human factors for successfully implementing and managing an eMedicines Management Programme

  • Participation of end-users in eMedicine system implementation and management
  • eMedicines team must be multi-disciplinary
  • Quality assurance and change management
  • Training and Clinical Support

Participation of end-users in eMedicine

All health professionals using the system to be part of implementation and maintanance of eMedicine systems

Why is this important in NZ health context?

“Best of breed” systems (as currently implemented) require striking a balance improved workload and medication safety.

Finding the right balanace can only be done with end-user participation and feedback

eMedicines team as a multi-disciplinary workforce

Emedicine team must contain non-physician healthcare professionals, health information professionals, managers, physicians and technical staff

Why is this important in NZ health context?

eMedication is a complex multi-disciplinary field which interacts with both clinical and technical issues and team needs skills to solve both

Robust system for quality assurance and change management

Information errors in eMedicines systems are generated from:

  1. Fragmented information (lack of interoperability)
  2. Interface problems between humans and machines

Why is this important in NZ health context?

eMedicine systems strives to make a complex system into a complicated process.

Reducing complexity needs QA and change management for risk reduction.

Training and Clinical Support

  1. Training programme, ensuring all users can use the system proficiently
  2. Clinical support for any questions and difficulties available 24/7

Why is this important in NZ health context?

Need to ensure that all users can use the system to minimise clinical risk.

Technical factors for successfully implementing and managing an eMedicines Management Programme

  • Technial support
  • Device maintanance
  • Interoperability and reporting

Technical support

  1. Prompt resolution and technical support
  2. System needs to be online 24/7 with redundancy backups

Why is this important in NZ health context?

Need to ensure that all users can access the system with minimal downtime.

Goal of less than 6 minutes downtime per year.

Device maintanance

  1. Adequate number of devices for users to use system
  2. Access to printers

Why is this important in NZ health context?

Need to ensure that all users can readily access devices and not impede their workflow.

Information on all devices must be rendered correctly.

Interoperability and reporting

  1. Interoperability with other systems leads to improved workload efficiency and actively contributes to medication safety.
  2. Reporting allows for improved quality assurance and change management in system

Why is this important in NZ health context?

Enahnces a positive feedback loop for workload, medication safety and quality assurance of the emedication system.
